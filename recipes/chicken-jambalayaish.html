Method (timed)

Start the stopwatch now.

  1. T+00:00
    Prep everything

    Dice all veg as listed. Slice the black pudding into the thinnest strips you can manage without breaking it. Lay everything out before turning the heat on.

    Why?

    This dish moves quickly once heat is applied. If you pause later to chop, you either burn aromatics or overcook protein. The thin black pudding strips are essential: increased surface area lets fat render and crisp before the interior collapses. Thicker pieces melt into greasy paste once exposed to moisture.

  2. T+05:00
    Crisp the black pudding

    Fry the strips in a little oil over medium heat until properly crisp. Remove to a plate; leave the fat behind.

    Why?

    Black pudding contains both fat and soluble flavour compounds. Rendering it early extracts those compounds into the cooking fat, which then becomes the flavour base for the entire dish. Removing it now prevents it absorbing liquid later and losing texture.

  3. T+10:00
    Base veg

    Add onion, celery and pepper with a pinch of salt. Cook until softened and sweet, not browned hard.

    Why?

    This stage converts raw sulphurous aromatics into sweetness. The salt draws out moisture, accelerating softening and preventing burning. Undercooking here leaves a raw vegetal edge that no amount of seasoning fixes later.

  4. T+17:00
    Aromatics

    Add garlic, smoked paprika and early chillies. Stir constantly for 30‚Äì60 seconds until fragrant.

    Why?

    Garlic and paprika both contain compounds that go bitter when overheated. Brief contact with hot fat blooms flavour without degradation. Smell is the correct cue here: acrid aroma means you overshot it.

  5. T+18:30
    Chicken

    Add chicken and stir until just sealed on the outside.

    Why?

    Sealing firms the exterior proteins so they withstand simmering later. Fully browning would dry the meat; skipping this stage leaves it flabby. You are setting texture, not cooking it through.

  6. T+22:00
    Rice & stock

    Stir in rice, then add stock to just cover. Gentle simmer, lid mostly on.

    Why?

    Coating the rice in fat limits surface starch release, improving texture. Gentle simmer ensures controlled absorption. Vigorous boiling causes uneven hydration: gluey bottom, dry top.

  7. T+35:00
    Finish

    Off the heat, add butter and a small hit of acid. Adjust seasoning. Fold black pudding through at the last second and serve.

    Why?

    Butter emulsifies residual starch and rounds mouthfeel. Acid restores brightness lost during simmering. Folding the black pudding in at the end preserves the crisp texture you deliberately created earlier.

Taste cues

  • Mid-cook: savoury, rounded aroma; no sharp edges.
  • Before serving: dull = acid, sharp = fat.
