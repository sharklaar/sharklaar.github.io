Method (timed)

Start the stopwatch now.

  1. T+00:00
    Prep everything

    Dice all veg as listed. From the black pudding, slice most into small thin chunks. Reserve a small portion and slice it into the thinnest strips you can manage without breaking. Get everything laid out before turning the heat on.

    Why?

    This dish has short windows where hesitation ruins things: aromatics bloom fast, chicken goes rubbery if you overdo it, and rice hydration is unforgiving. Prep first means you‚Äôre executing, not reacting.

    Splitting the black pudding gives you two different textural roles: chunky bits to fold through for savoury depth across the whole dish, and ultra-thin strips that can crisp hard and survive as a garnish. Treat it all the same and you either lose crunch or lose distribution.

  2. T+05:00
    Crisp the black pudding

    Fry all the black pudding pieces in a little oil over medium heat until crisp. Remove to a plate. Separate the ultra-thin strips and keep them pristine for garnish. Keep the fat in the pan.

    Why?

    Rendering black pudding early gives you a fat rich in savoury compounds that seasons everything cooked in it afterwards. That‚Äôs deeper than ‚Äúadd more salt later‚Äù.

    The chunkier pieces can be folded through the rice later for distribution. The ultra-thin strips crisp harder and faster ‚Äî keeping them separate preserves a brittle crunch you can‚Äôt recover once they soften.

    This stage removes harsh raw flavours and replaces them with sweetness and body. If you rush it, the finished dish always tastes slightly unfinished.
